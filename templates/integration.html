integral formulas

Integration is a fundamental concept in calculus that is used to find the accumulation of quantities such as area, volume, displacement, work, and probability. It is often considered the reverse process of differentiation because integrating a derivative returns the original function (plus a constant). There are two main types of integration: indefinite integration, which produces a family of functions and includes the constant of integration (C), and definite integration, which calculates a numerical value between specified limits. To evaluate a definite integral, first find the antiderivative of the function, then substitute the upper limit and lower limit into the antiderivative and subtract the two results according to the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. Integration is widely used in mathematics, physics, engineering, economics, and data science to solve problems involving continuous change and accumulated quantities.
